Back in 2007, Mercedes model nomenclatures still corresponded more or less with the size of the engine under the hood, and so it was with the first of the Mercedes-AMG C63 Sedan generations, which had a 451-hp 6.2L V8 under the hood. There were year-on-year changes to the Mercedes-AMG C63 Sedan, including a new seven-speed automated manual transmission for MY2012. The W204 Mercedes-AMG C63 underwent a redesign for the 2014 model year, with the C63’s charismatic naturally aspirated 6.2L V8 discontinued in favor of a twin-turbocharged 4.0L V8, still dubbed the C63, and now with 469 hp. The fifth-generation redesign of the Mercedes-AMG C63 sedan from model years 2024 did away with V8s altogether; the C63 is now a hybridized 2.0L turbo-four with 671 hp.


The redesigned W206 is the first of the Mercedes-AMG C63 Sedan generations to have only turbocharged 2.0L four-cylinder engines across the board, culminating in the hybridized C63 E Performance with 671 hp and 752 lb-ft of torque.


To prepare it for its last three years on sale, the W205 received a mild facelift, but the C63 powertrain remained untouched.


The operatic naturally aspirated 6.2L V8 didn’t survive the transition to the W205 and was replaced with a twin-turbocharged 4.0L V8 with 469 hp (503 hp in the S). It remained in use in the previous-generation coupe.
