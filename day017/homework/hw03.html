Founded by former Mercedes engineers, Erhard Melcher and Hans Werner, AMG is the legendary performance division of Mercedes-Benz, credited for creating some of the most iconic vehicles to date, including the 1999 SL 73 which featured a V-12 engine, the E 55, and the GT R. The founders of AMG had a dream of creating a sedan that could achieve 300-kMh (186 MPH) and saw their vision begin to manifest in late 1984 when the Mercedes-Benz W124 made leaps and bounds in terms of automotive design and quality. Featuring state-of-the-art multi-link suspension and the most aerodynamic design of the time, AMG promptly began working on a V-8 conversion of the W124. The result was a vehicle that would set the precedent for future super sedans, as well as spearhead the entire movement.
